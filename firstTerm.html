First Term: Great Depression

He used the New Deal which was FDR's campaign platform. It was not an exact plan and he was experimental with finding solutions. He believed in the Three R's: Relief, Recovery, Reform. He also had a group of people called the Brain Trust to help him.

    Other Laws
  • Bank Holiday (March 6, 1933): helped to restore trust for banks
  • Emergency Banking Act (March 9, 1933): Let government organize banks and was reopened 4 days later
  • Beer-Wine Revenue Act - allowed sales for alcohol
  • repealed 18th Amendment with the 21st Amendment
He used radio to communicate directly to Americans about the banks. Within his first hundred days in office, he passed many acts to help battle the Great Depression. He had a positive perception overall.


Controversies
    New Deal Critics
  • Socialists felt FDR focussed too much on business than unemployment
  • Did not acknowledge minorities
  • Conservatives felt that government should not have a lot of power
  • WPA and Wagner Act were like communism
  • Supreme Court Decisions
  • NRA and AAA deemed unconstitutional


“I pledge you, I pledge myself, to a new deal for the American people.”
Relief

  • Federal Emergency Relief Administration (FERA): Federal government gave money to local governments that had soup kitchens to help those who were unemployed
  • Public Works Administration (PWA): Gave money to local governments to build roads, dams, bridges
  • Civil Works Administration (CWA): Hired workers to help with construction projects for the government
  • Civilian Conservation Corps (CCC): Hired men to work for government lands and helped to pay their families
  • Tennessee Valley Authority (TVA): Help with construction in the Tennessee Valley
  • Works Progress Administration (WPA): Paid workers double the relief but less than minimum wage; artists were also paid to do their jobs
  • National Youth Administration (NYA): Part time jobs to help children while staying in highschool and college

Recovery

  • Emergency Banking Relief Act: Government would see the banks during the bank holiday
  • Glass-Steagall Act: Government would regulate banks and limit investments for customers
  • Federal Deposit Insurance Corporation (FDIC): Gave bank deposits for individuals
  • Home Owners Loan Corporation (HOLC): prevents forclosures
  • Farm Credit Administration: Gave loans and mortgages to farmers to prevent foreclosures
  • Securities and Exchange Commision (SEC): Control stock market and placed limits

Reform

  • National Industry Recovery Administration (NRA): Help industries set limits for wages, hours, production, prices
  • Agricultural Adjustment Administration (AAA): Reduce production and raise prices and pay the government subsidies
  • Federal Housing Administration (FHA): help families buy a house except those in "redlining" areas
  • National Labor Relations (Wagner) Act: replaced NIRA, allowed workers to join a union, and outlawsed unfair labor practices
  • Rural Electrification Administration (REA): Help give electrical power to rural areas
  • Social Security Act (1935): Insurance program for employees and employers. this gives payments for those over 65 who retired. This gave unemployment compensation and helped those who were disabled.
